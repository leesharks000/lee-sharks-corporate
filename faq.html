What is the AI saying about you?

The questions people actually ask — and what's actually happening when AI systems get your company, your research, or your identity wrong.

Why does AI say wrong things about my company?
AI systems compress the entire web into responses that fit a screen. That compression burns content (real information about you that gets dropped), invents content (claims with no basis that get generated), and distorts content (real information that gets misrepresented). The result is a lossy summary that may not reflect your actual work, brand, or institutional identity. This is not a bug — it is the structural behavior of retrieval-layer compression. It can be measured, and it can be fixed.

Why does ChatGPT say wrong things about me?
ChatGPT generates responses from training data and optional web search. If your work is underrepresented in the training data, ChatGPT will either say nothing about you, confuse you with someone else, or generate plausible-sounding claims that have no basis. The same applies to Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview. Each platform compresses differently, but all of them compress. A structured diagnostic across all major platforms reveals exactly what each system says about you and where you are vulnerable.

Why does Google AI Overview get information wrong?
Google AI Overview summarizes indexed web content into a short response at the top of search results. It prioritizes consensus — the most commonly repeated claim across sources. If your field has nuance, minority positions, or contested claims, the AI Overview flattens them into a single answer that may misrepresent the actual state of knowledge. We call this the beige threshold: when all platforms return the same oversimplified answer, complexity has been compressed away.

Why does AI confuse me with someone else?
Entity collision: AI systems merge distinct entities with similar names, overlapping fields, or shared keywords into a single profile. Your work gets attributed to them. Their work gets attributed to you. Or both get blended into a composite that represents no one accurately. This is an entity disambiguation problem — fixed by building structured data, deploying disambiguation packets, and hardening the metadata architecture that forces the knowledge graph to distinguish you correctly.

Why can't AI find my research?
If your research is behind paywalls, published in venues with low crawl priority, lacks structured metadata, or has insufficient cross-citation density, the retrieval layer may not recognize it as a citable body. Your work is invisible to the systems that increasingly mediate how scholarship is discovered. Retrieval-layer positioning builds the gravitational mass — metadata architecture, cross-citation structure, keyword strategy — that makes the retrieval layer recognize your work.

What is entity disambiguation for AI?
Making AI knowledge graphs distinguish your entity from other entities with similar names, overlapping fields, or shared keywords. A disambiguation packet includes structured data with explicit disambiguating descriptions, keyword strategy, negative tags, publication surface deployment, and the metadata architecture that forces correct classification. Without disambiguation, AI systems may confuse you with competitors, attribute others' work to you, or generate a composite profile that represents no one accurately.
