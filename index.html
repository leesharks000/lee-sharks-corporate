About

I'm a teacher in Detroit with a PhD in Comparative Literature. I teach 10th-grade World Literature during the day. In the margins of my school schedule, I built the Crimson Hexagonal Archive — 480+ DOI-anchored deposits on CERN's Zenodo repository — as a decade-long experiment in making meaning survive compression.

Along the way I founded Compression Studies as a discipline, built the Encyclotron as a measurement instrument, published Meaning Feudalism as a platform governance critique, and launched four distributed journals.

Everything I offer clients comes from tools I built and tested on my own work. The archive is not a credential — it is the laboratory. The 480+ deposits are not publications in the legacy sense — they are permanent, citable, DOI-anchored experiments in retrieval-layer survival.
